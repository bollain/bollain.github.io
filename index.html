Born and raised in Mexico, but proud to call Vancouver and Canada my home. I love everything about this city, its vibrant beer culture, close proximity to the great outdoors, and its budding technology industry.

I have a background in Finance and Economics, and spent over five years working in corporate lending and underwriting with some of the largest players in Canada and the world. Though I made some great friendships along the way, I found that my heart was not in it for the long run, which led to one of the toughest choices in my life.

I am now in my final year of the Bachelor of Computer Science at the University of British Columbia, where I have found my true calling as a computer scientist. I love programming and problem solving; there is nothing more satisfying than turning an idea into a finished product.

In my free time I enjoy cycling, rock climbing in Squamish, brewing beer, and hanging out with my feline friend Miguelito. On a rainy Vancouver afternoon you can probably find me wrangling code at a local coffee shop.

H2GO

This web-app allows users to find drinking fountains and bike racks in the city of Vancouver. Users can create their profiles and save their favorite drinking fountains and bike racks, and can tweet whenever they are using the app. The application was built using Ruby on Rails, and uses open data sets provided by the City of Vancouver. Check it out here: H2GO

MeetUp

An app for Android that allows students to map the most efficient routes for their classes, and gives them the option to determine the the optimal place to meet, based on their schedules and distance from each other. The app uses the FourSquare API to find places to meet within the given MeetUp radius. Developed using Android Studio.

BeerApp

BeerApp is a simple java program that suggests food pairings for your favorite beer. The user types in their favorite beer, and the app queries several databases to suggest food pairings and recipes for the perfect meal.
